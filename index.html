Summer Mini-Camp

Summary:
Senior students used basic science principles from Newton’s second law of motion (F = m × a) to design and build rockets to understand how rockets are propelled into the atmosphere. They also built a launch pad using PVC pipes. By pumping air through the PVC pipes and using water pressure, they were able to launch their rockets.

Junior students learned about cars and used balloons to propel them using Newton’s third law of motion (for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction).

Topics covered: Math, Physics, Engineering, Presentation Skills, Teamwork, Competition — and FUN!




2024


Winter Mini-Camp

Summary: Varsity students learned about architectural design, then used their knowledge to build bridges strong enough to support various objects. We also completed an LED Arduino project, using an LED Matrix board, wires, and C++ code. Junior varsity students learned about states of matter using dry ice, liquid nitrogen, hot plates, and balloons. They experimented with paper planes to observe Bernoulli’s Principle. And tested different powders with various liquids to determine what they are. I'm the end, there was a big competition with the bridges and they made ice cream in a bag!
Topics covered: Presentation Skills, Physics, Chemistry, Architectural Design, Coding, Competition, and Fun!
